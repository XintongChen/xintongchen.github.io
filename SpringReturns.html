Returns (Mobile)

Spring is a fashion and beauty marketplace where people can discover and buy products from 1500+ brands.

Why

Spring launched the new desktop Returns experience mid 2018. Two months after the launch, we've got lots of positive feedback and saw a 10% increase in NPS for customers who have experienced a refund, as well as, a 20% reduction in tickets from users requesting a return label.

There are about 50% of Spring users who shop in our iOS app. So launching a better mobile Returns experience will definitely benefit users and drive positive impact on user retention.


Challenges

Mobile Friendly

From desktop to iOS, it was not a direct translation from the big screen to small screen. So figuring out how to prioritize information and use interaction/animation to avoid information overloading is the top challenge.

Multiple Returns Flows

Users can purchase from 1500+ brands on Spring, and different brands have different return policies. So figuring out how to guide users through 3 different return flows in a consistant way is the other challenge.


Design and Iteration

The design and iteration process lasted for about 3 weeks. I gathered feedback from internal stakeholders and polished the design for a few rounds.

Here are 2 examples of my design iteration:

Example 1: Iteration of Enter-Tracking-Number Page.
V1 has everything on one page. After I realized the problem of information overloading, I started to think about how to break it into multiple pieces and have users focus on one thing at a time.
V2 and V3 allow users to enter tracking number in a pop-up modal or a second page. From V2 to V3, I gave users an easier access to the keyboard which better serviced the goal of allowing users to enter tracking numbers easily.
Example 2: Iteration of Your-Item Page.
V1 expands item information but pushes all CTA further down the page, which requires users to make more effort when they want to take an action on the item. After I realized this problem, I collapsed the information section and only presented part of it at the beginning (V2). Further thinking about the use case of this page, I cannot justify the #1 goal of this page is to check item information. So I put all CTAs upfront and moved item information on a separate page (V3).

Next Steps

Right now, users have to manually enter their tracking numbers to track their returns, which isn't the most pleasant experience for users and may cause potential typos/mistakes. As a next step, we will build a feature that allows users to scan their tracking numbers more easily in the App.
