Grovo Style Guide



Grovo is workplace learning platform based on Microlearning theory.

2017 summer, I built and maintained design style guide for Grovo Product Design team, which improved product design consistency and efficiency.

Read Full Style Guide

Problem

The product kept evolving very fast in the past 2 years, and there were multiple designers working on different parts of the product. So it was hard to keep it consistent.

Goal

To build a style guide for product design at Grovo, which allows for better product development cycles and also aims to improve the user experience of the product.

Research

Rules

First of all, I focused on the very basic rules of our product design, including responsive grid system, colors, typography, icons and writing styles.

Conventions

Building a style guide is not all about creating new components or interactions. A lot of times, I looked for the best treatment for each component, built a convention, and clarified when to use this convention.

For example, we use tooltips to provide hints and simple instructions. Even though they were all very well-functioning, but they had either different radio corners, drop-shadows, or paddings:

Sketch templates

The outcome of this project includes a pdf version guidance and a Sketch template with all the components. Designers can grab and use these compnents which improves our efficiency and consistency a lot.

Maintenance

Building style guide is definitely not a one time work. As we are growing our products, the style guide is continually expanding and reshaping as well.
