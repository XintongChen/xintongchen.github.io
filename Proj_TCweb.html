TC Websites Redesign & Development

Redeisgn and develop TC websites within T4 Content Management System, to cater needs of differnt academic departments

Site Name: Office of International Affairs will be published soon

Requirements: (1) Change the TC blue to a warmer theme color. (2) Use flip card to display alumni information. (3) Optimize card layout.

Solution: (1) Dark red and blak/grey were chosen as theme color. (2) New content layout (flip-card) was created in T4 system to present alumni information. (3) 2-cards layout was used instead of 3-cards layout, to make the page looks balanced.

Site Name: Campaign for Educational Equity will be published soon

Requirements: (1) They wanted to add more visuals and colors, because there are too much text in their old website. (2) Redesign the home page layout, but at the same time keep its original style.

Solution: (1) Some brighter colors and images were added on homepage. (2) The layout and information structure were redesigned, but the original banner was kept.

Site Name: Center for Technology & School Change will be published soon

Requirements: (1) Add background videos in primary carousel. (2) A different layout and color palette from the traditional TC websites. (3) Put logo and slogan on the homepage.

Solution: (1) New content type was created in T4 system to insert video as primary carousel background. (2) A new color palette was chosen based on their original site. (3) Header and primary carousel were redesigned to display logo and slogan.

Site Name: Nursing Education view website

Requirements: (1) Put impressive pictures on the homepage. (2) Add buttons that link to external pages.

Solution: (1) Pictures closely related to their topic were chosen to catch people's eyes. (2) Buttons were added on the upper-right corner and consisitent with the TC web style.

Description

I work in a group of 4 to redesign and develop Teachers College websites, following the popular web design trends, e.g. cards layout, hero image, flat design, responsive design, mobile-first and so on. We develop sites within T4 Content Management System. We take advantages of T4, as well as get out of the restrictions of T4. When redesigning these websites, we satisfy the personalized requirements of different departments and also keep them consistent with the TC style.
