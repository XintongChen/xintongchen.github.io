Spring Design Explorations

Spring is a fashion and beauty marketplace where people can discover and buy products from 1500+ brands.

During my first 3 months at Spring, I led design for multiple features across pre-purchase and post-purchase experience. Two of them got launched recently: mobile returns and claiming Spring credits. I wanted to share my design process of these 2 features.

Feature 1: Claiming Spring Credits

SpringBack

At the beginning of 2018, Spring launched a loyalty program, SpringBack, aiming to increase user rentention. SpringBack gives users 4% cash back for any purchase.

6 months after SpringBack launched, we got lots of feedbacks from user research and analytical dashbaord. Two outstanding feedbacks are:

  1. More than 50% of Spring users assume that SpringBack offers shopping credit instead of real cash.

  2. Cash Back that values more than $7 is more compelling for users. We saw a deep increase of claiming rate from users who have more than $7 cash back.


Business Goal

By offering users an option to claim their cash as credits with a bonus, we wanted to give users more compelling rewards and drive impact on user retention (Spring's #1 goal).


Design & Iteration

Top challegne for this design is to clearly communicate the deifference between claiming cash and credits and support users to make a decision easily.

I landed on the first version of the design, based on the design requirement that I should keep credit-claiming consistent with the existing cash-claiming feature.

Next Steps

How much bonus should we provide?

We are launching 50% bonus and 25% bonus for claiming credit to 2 different groups users and see which options has more impact on user retention. Because providing high rewards for users could be a potential risk of giving away more money than we need to.

Cash or credit?

We also want to see whether users prefer cash back or shopping credits, and then ajust the focus of SpringBack accordingly.

Feature 2: Returns Flows (Mobile)

Why

Spring lauched the new desktop Returns experience mid 2018. Two months after launch, we've got lots of positive feedback and see a 10% increase in NPS for customers who have experienced a refund, as well as, a 20% reduction in tickets from users requesting a return label.

There are about 50% Spring users who shop in our iOS app. So lauching a better mobile Returns experience will definitely benefit users and drive positive impact on user retention.


Challenges

Mobile Friendly

From desktop to iOS, it was not a direct translation from big screen to small scree. Mirroing the desktop experience on iOS, there is limited space, So how to prioritize information and use interaction/animation to deliver content to avoid information overloading is the top challenge.

Multiple Returns Process

Users can purchase from 1500+ brands on Spring, and different brands have different return processes. There are 3 different returns flows.


Design and Iteration

The design and iteration process lasted for about 3 weeks. I polished the design for a few rounds and gathered feedback from engineers, marketing, and other internal stakeholders to iterate the design.

Here are 2 examples of the iteration of Enter-Tracking-Number.

Example 1: Iteration of Enter-Tracking-Number Page.
V1 has everything on one page. After I realized the problem of information overloading, I started to think about thow to break it into multiple pieces and have users focus on one thing each time.
V2 and V3 allow users to enter tracking number in a pop-up modal or a second page. From V2 to V3, I gave users an easier access to the keyboard which better services the goal of allowing users to enter tracking numbers easily.
Example 2: Iteration of Your-Item Page.
V1 expands item information but pushes all CTA further down the page, which requires users to make more effort if they want to take an action on the item. After I realized this problem, I collapsed the information section and only presented part of it at the beginning (V2). Further thinking about the use case of this page, I cannot justify the #1 goal of this page it to check item information. So I put all CTAs upfront, moved item information on a separate page, and landed on V3.
