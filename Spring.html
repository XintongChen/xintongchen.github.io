Spring Case Studies

Spring is a fashion and beauty marketplace where people can discover and buy products from 1500+ brands.

I've led the end-to-end design process for 2 features, mobile returns and claiming Spring credits. Both of them will get launched soon. And I wanted to share my design process of these 2 features.

Case Study 1: Mobile Returns

Why

Spring lauched the new desktop Returns experience mid 2018. Two months after launch, we've got lots of positive feedback and see a 10% increase in NPS for customers who have experienced a refund, as well as, a 20% reduction in tickets from users requesting a return label.

There are about 50% Spring users who shop in our iOS app. So lauching a better mobile Returns experience will definitely benefit users and drive positive impact on user retention.

Challenge

From desktop to iOS, it was not a direct translation from big screen to small scree. When mirroing the desktop experience on iOS, here is limited space. So how to prioritize information and use interaction/animation to deliver content to avoid information overloading is the top challenge.

Design and Iteration

The UI&UX design and iteration process lasted for about 3 weeks. I've gathered feedback from engineers, marketing, and other internal stakeholders, and iterated the design based on their comments.

Here is the prototype for 1 of the 3 different returns flow: https://bit.ly/2McuyL1

Next Steps

Right now, users have to manually enter their tracking numbers to track their returns, which isn't the most pleasant experience for users and may cause potential typo/mistakes. As next step, we will build a tracking number scanning feature that allows users to enter their tracking numbers much easier in the App.
