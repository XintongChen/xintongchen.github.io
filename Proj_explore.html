Grovo Explore

A tour of Grovo for potential buyers

What's Grovo

Grovo is a B2B SaaS company that delivers engaging Microlearning® lessons and platform to help employees do their jobs better.

Problem

The product was very gated: Grovo didn't provide an engaging and easy way for potential buyers to better understand Grovo's Microlearning solution.

Idea potential buyer's journey vs. Grovo potential buyer's journey
https://www.grovo.com/learn-more

The Request-A-Demo form was too serious for early stage potential buyers due to the high number of fields required

Data from Google Analytics shows only 15% of visitors that land on the Learn More page actually submit their information to request a demo.

Goal

Design a frictionless, engaging, and lightweight product tour to:

1. introduce Microlearning solution to potential buyers and differentiate Grovo from the other learning products

2. make the product less gated and attract more serious buyers for the Sales team

Constraints

At the time, we had limited engineering resources. Therefore, we only utilized Front End resources for this project and decided not to support a mobile experience.

Research and findings

Firstly, I conducted research on product tours. Here is a collection of different product tours I went through: http://bit.ly/2BgBi8Q

Through my research there are mainly 2 types of product tours:

1. Guided tour: Users follow the step-by-step instructions to go through the highlighted features.

2. Free exploration: Users explore the product at their own pace. Instructions will show up whenever the user is looking at a feature for the the first time.

To keep the product tour short and engaging, I assumed we shouldn't open the entire product and should only showcase the key differentiators.

But what features should be showcased? Is my assumption right? I talked to the Sales and Customer Success teams by asking the following questions:

SALES TEAM

What features does a potential buyer look for when evaluating a new learning product?

CUSTOMER SUCCESS TEAM

What features excite our current customers the most?

These two teams gave me the exact same answers:

1. Watching Microlearning lessons

2. Browsing the Microlearning Library

3. Creating Microlearning content

Our Sales team validated my assumption as well; there is no need to open the entire product to potential buyers. Complicated features, such as user management and reporting, are not Grovo's strengths and therefore should not be highlighted in the tour.

User flow

Because creating Microlearning content is harder than browsing the Microlearning Library and watching Microlearning lessons. I decided to guide people to go through these 3 features from easy to hard.

Next steps

The metric we use to measure the success of this project is:

The increase in conversion rates of leads to prospects

We soft launched Grovo Explore in late January. We will get more data to show the result later.

After we get enough visitors of Grovo Explore, we also wanted to use the data to answer the following questions:
