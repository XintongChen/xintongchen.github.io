SpringBack - Credit Rewards

Spring is an e-commerce marketplace where people can discover and buy products from 1500+ brands.

I led design for Credit Rewards which got launched in 2019 fall. I wanted to share my design process here.

What's SpringBack & Why Credit Rewards

At the beginning of 2018, Spring launched a loyalty program, SpringBack, aiming to increase user retention. SpringBack gives users 4% cash back for any purchase.

6 months after SpringBack launched, we got lots of feedbacks from user research and analytical dashboard. Two surprising feedbacks are:

  1. More than 50% of Spring users assume that SpringBack offers shopping credit instead of real cash.

  2. Cash back above $7 is more compelling for users. We saw a large increase in claiming rate from users who have more than $7 cash back.


Business Goal

By offering users an option to claim their SpringBack as credits with a bonus, we wanted to give users more compelling rewards and drive impact on user retention (Spring's #1 goal).


Design & Iteration

The top challenge for this design is to clearly communicate the difference between claiming cash vs. credits and support users to make a decision easily.

Next Steps

How much bonus should we provide?

We are launching 50% bonus and 25% bonus for claiming credit to 2 different user groups. We wanted to see which options will bring more benefits to the business and find the balance between giving rewards and increasing user retention.

Cash or credit?

We also want to see whether users prefer cash back or shopping credits, and then adjust SpringBack accordingly.
