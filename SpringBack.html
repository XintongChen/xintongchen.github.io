SpringBack - Credit Rewards

Spring is a fashion and beauty marketplace where people can discover and buy products from 1500+ brands.

I led design for Credit Rewards which got launched in 2019 fall. I wanted to share my design process here.

What's SpringBack & Why Credit Rewards

At the beginning of 2018, Spring launched a loyalty program, SpringBack, aiming to increase user rentention. SpringBack gives users 4% cash back for any purchase.

6 months after SpringBack launched, we got lots of feedbacks from user research and analytical dashboard. Two outstanding feedbacks are:

  1. More than 50% of Spring users assume that SpringBack offers shopping credit instead of real cash.

  2. Cash Back that values more than $7 is more compelling for users. We saw a deep increase of claiming rate from users who have more than $7 cash back.


Business Goal

By offering users an option to claim their cash as credits with a bonus, we wanted to give users more compelling rewards and drive impact on user retention (Spring's #1 goal).


Design & Iteration

Top challenge for this design is to clearly communicate the deifference between claiming cash and credits and support users to make a decision easily.

Next Steps

How much bonus should we provide?

We are launching 50% bonus and 25% bonus for claiming credit to 2 different groups users and see which options has more impact on user retention. Because providing high rewards for users could be a potential risk of giving away more money than we need to.

Cash or credit?

We also want to see whether users prefer cash back or shopping credits, and then ajust the focus of SpringBack accordingly.
