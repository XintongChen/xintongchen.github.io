to raise people’s awareness of Ebola and eliminate people’s threat of Ebola.

Description

The threat of Ebola — an extremely dangerous and deadly viral infection — is very real. Though the disease is largely constrained to the other side of the planet, we are always very aware that Ebola is only a plane–ride away.

But â€¦ Is that really going to happen? How easy is it to spread the infection, and under what circumstances is it likely — or unlikely — to reach your doorstep? How does Ebola as a disease take advantage of those natural human connections to wreak havoc? Using those same connections, between one person and another, how can we fight back against Ebola through treatment and prevention? How can helping others actually help yourself?

In Ebola!, you manipulate the world in a way you have never seen before. You have to think about how disease spreads, and how it takes advantage of the vulnerabilities associated with human interconnectedness. But you may also choose to use those connections for good, to spread awareness, prevention, and treatment to those in greatest need

Ebola! educates players about Ebola using situated meanings, where they learn about the real life scenarios of Ebola through simulation. Simulation provides players an opportunity to interact with other players and visualize how Ebola can travel. The game is over when Ebola has reached “YOU” on a player’s board, so each player’s actions and decisions co-design the game that results in the uncertainty during simulation.

Iterative Design

We began our design process by identifying the intended goals of Ebola!. We wanted our game to show the interconnectedness in the spread of disease. Our main concern was to inexplicitly and explicitly show linkage. We debated on the pros and cons of having a cooperative or competitive game and the aesthetic purpose of the board and tangible elements. As we playtested and refined our game, the goal and cooperative/competitive value of the game changed.
