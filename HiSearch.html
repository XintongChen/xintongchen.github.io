High Intent Search

Redesign of e-commerce high intent search

My Contribution

I am leading the design for hi-intent search from concept to delivery across desktop, mobile, and iOS. There are 6 people in our Search & Discover team: 1 user researcher, 1 product manager, 1 tech lead, 2 software engineer, and 1 product designer (myself).

Why improve search?

What’s Spring & Spring’s business goal

Spring is a beauty and fashion marketplace targeting to millennials. This year, the #1 business goal of Spring is to increase user retention. We wanted to drive our users to come back to Spring after their first purchase and make their 2nd, 3rd, … purchase.

Why improve search

Spring carries 1500+ brands and 200k+ products, so an easy-to-use and accurate search is critical to increase search-to-cart conversion and then increase user retention.

User goal

We’ve already heard some complains, like the search is not accurate, the search results are overwhelmed, it takes too long to find the right product … So, for our users, we want to make the search more accurate and smarter and help our users to find what they are looking for easily.

Define vision

Led by my PM, we had a one-day offsite with team members and internal stakeholders. During offsite, we as a team used Spring search, understood our current limitations/pain points, researched competitive landscape, and formulated short and medium term goals for the team.

Vision

After offsite, we better defined our vision for the Search & Discovery initiative:

1. search and browse are different, search is high-intent, browse is low-intent

2. priorities: refinements, search bar & navigation bar, browse/suggestion

Success Metrics

The ultimate success metrics is search-to-cart conversion, at the same time, we will watch the change of search-to-PDP conversion and search-to-love conversion as well.

Understand search behavior

User research

I worked with our user researcher and interviewed 20 people. We tested both desktop and mobile search experience.

During the user research process, I provided search tasks to make sure we can get the most from observing users’ search experience. As the user interviews moving on, I started to build some assumptions and revised research questions/tasks based previous interviews and my assumptions.

Quantitive input

At the same time, I looked at data dashboard (Amplitude) to understand the problem from a quantitive perspective.

% of users that refine their search by different filters.

From high to low:
Brands 8%, On Sale 6%, Price 3%, Size 3%, New Arrival 0%
% of users that search and refine on different devices.

Takeaways

1. Users are using some filters more often than the others. Generally speaking, users refine their search by brands and sales more than size.

2. In terms of devices, our users search on desktop and mobile, but there are more users who refine their search on desktop.
