High Intent Search

Redesign of e-commerce high intent search exerience

Spring is a fashion and beauty marketplace where people can discover and buy products from 1500+ brands.

Working with a team of 6 talented people, I'm leading the design from concept to delivery for High Intent Search across dektop, mobile, and iOS.

Why improve search

For Spring

The #1 business goal of Spring is to increase user retention. Search is the primary mechanism for achieving Spring's business goal. Because, by early 2018, over 110k unique users already use Spring's search each month, so even a modest increase of search-to-cart conversion will drive significant impact on user retention.

For users

Search is core to an online shopping experience. Especially when there are 200k+ products on Spring, we want to make it easier for users to find what they are looking for and instill the confidence of starting style search on Spring.

Define vision

Search & Discover squad had a one-day offsite with team members and internal stakeholders. During offsite, the entire team used Spring search, understood our current limitations/pain points, researched competitive landscape, and formulated short and medium term goals for the team.

Vision

After offsite, we better defined our vision for the Search & Discovery initiative:

1. Search and browse are different, search is high-intent, browse is low-intent;

2. Priorities: high intent search (refinements, search bar, and navigation bar), browse/suggestion

Success Metrics

The ultimate success metrics is search-to-cart conversion, at the same time, we will watch search-to-PDP conversion and search-to-love conversion as well.

High Intent Search

After we defined the vision for the Search & Discover initiative, I started the design process for high intent search from understanding search behavior.

Understand search behavior

User Interview

I worked with our user researcher and conducted user interview with 10 Spring users and 9 non-Spring users. We asked questions to understand their shopping preference and tested Spring's search exerience on both desktop and mobile.

During the user interview process, I provided initial questions and search tasks. As the user interviews moved on, I started to build some assumptions and revised research questions/tasks based previous interviews and my assumptions.

Screenshot of some search/refine tasks for users

After user research was done. I synthesized the interviews in Trello and found out the common themes in desktop and mobile search experience.

Screenshot of research synthesis

Take-aways

Nav Bar vs. Serach Bar: Most users start search from Nav Bar and don’t trust Search Bar generally on all shopping sites, but users do use Search Bar when searching with high intent.

Category Tree: Some users don’t expect to utilize Category Tree to refine their search or ignore it.

Refinements: All users further refine their search by Color, Size, Price, or Brands. There are some usability issues with each filter that makes the refine experience not very smooth.

Sort: Users like to sort by Lowest Price, but they feel the search results are not very relevant when they sort by Lowest Price.

Fun Fact: Men prefer having limited options and getting search done quickly, while girls prefer further refining their search and getting the best deal.

Define Problem

While I was trying to understand users' search behovior from different perspectives, I felt very excited, because as I moved forward, my new findings could confirm my previous findings. After I connected the dots, I could see a whole picture of the problems in our Search and Refine experience.

Problems

1. Users don't trust query search.
Because search results are not accurate enough. Users still have to take extra effort to refine their search even though they've already described what they are looking for in the search queries.

2. Desktop refine (filter) experience is not intuitive enough.
There are different usability issues with different filters.

3. The structure of taxonomy tree is not perfect,
which makes some products less searchable/discoverable.

Technical Constraints

After the problems are clearly defined, we took our engineering resources into consideration to assess what problems we would work on at that time. We saw an opportunity to

1. Improve the accuracy and user experience of search.
Auto refine search results if there is color, brand, taxonomy or gener in search query.

2. Improve the refine experience on desktop.

3. Improve the design of Category refinement.
Even though we saw the problem with the structure of our taxonomoy tree, but we didn't have the budget to restructure it. We could only improve the UI to fix the problem temporarily.

High Intent Query Search

After engineers improved the search algorithm, we were able to provide very accurate results for users' high intent search. And then I started to figure out 2 UX problems that came up with the improved search functionality:

1. Search Bar & Navigation

Even though query search is the most natural way for high intent search, but from user interviews, we found out that users usually don't start their search from search bar because they don't trust search in general for online shopping sites. Our engineers improved the search algorithm and Spring was able to return very relevant items based on users’ search queries.

Design Challenge

So the design challenge I had was to fix the current usability issue with our search bar and get more users to start their search from search bar, this way users will find out that Spring provides very great query search experience, and in long term, people will get more confidence of doing fashion search in Spring.

Old search bar: 1. The search bar looks like a patch, some users don’t really see it; 2. The placement of navigation and search bar are awkward, lots of users acidentally touch the navigation and the navigation dropdown will block search bar.

Another challenge I had when designing High Intent Search experience was what’s the best hierarchy for all the refinements on search page.

I got 2 different inputs when I tried to figure out refinement hierarchy. Based on the data analysis, I saw there are more clicks on Price and Discount than Color and brands. But from user interviews, I found out people usually refine by brands and colors first, and then sizes, lastly price/discount. Because people don't want to limit the options at the beginning, and usually they don't have a clear bar for price.

2. Size refinement & My Sizes

Spring has My Sizes feature for all registered members, where users can set up sizes in their profiles and only see what’s in stock in their sizes. But users usually don’t notice it.

Screenshot of search results page: My Sizes and Size refinement are very sparate.
Most people used to look for refinements on the left side of the page, so they end up not seeing My Sizes.

Design Challenge

Redesign Size refinement and My Sizes to educate non Spring memebers or new users about My Sizes and make it easier to refine by size.

User Stories

I wrote down users stories to lead my design for different types of use cases.

As a Spring member who has My Sizes set up, when I am refining my search by size, I want to be able to turn on or off My Size, so that I will have more control over sizes.

As a new Spring member, when I am refining my search by size, I want to save my current size preference to My Sizes, so that I can get more personalized search results faster in the future.

As a non Spring member, when I am refining my search by size and seeing My Sizes for the first time, I want to know what’s My Sizes and how it helps me with my future search, so that it will be easier for me to make a decision on whether I want to set it up or not.
