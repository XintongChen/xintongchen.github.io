High Intent Search

Redesign of e-commerce high intent search exerience

Spring is a fashion and beauty marketplace where people can discover and buy products from 1500+ brands.

Working with a team of 6 talented people, I'm leading the design from concept to delivery for High Intent Search across dektop, mobile, and iOS.

Why improve search

For Spring

The #1 business goal of Spring is to increase user retention. Search is the primary mechanism for achieving Spring's business goal. Because, by early 2018, over 110k unique users already use Spring's search each month, so even a modest increase of search-to-cart conversion will drive significant impact on user retention.

For users

Search is core to an online shopping experience. Especially when there are 200k+ products on Spring, we want to make it easier for users to find what they are looking for and instill the confidence of starting style search on Spring.

Define vision

Led by my PM, we had a one-day offsite with team members and internal stakeholders. During offsite, the entire team used Spring search, understood our current limitations/pain points, researched competitive landscape, and formulated short and medium term goals for the team.

Vision

After offsite, we better defined our vision for the Search & Discovery initiative:

1. search and browse are different, search is high-intent, browse is low-intent

2. priorities: refinements, search bar & navigation bar, browse/suggestion

Success Metrics

The ultimate success metrics is search-to-cart conversion, at the same time, we will watch search-to-PDP conversion and search-to-love conversion as well.

Understand search behavior

User research

I worked with our user researcher and spoke to 10 Spring users and 10 non-Spring users. We tested our search exerience on both desktop and mobile.

During the user research process, I provided search tasks to make sure we can get the most from observing users’ search experience. As the user interviews moving on, I started to build some assumptions and revised research questions/tasks based previous interviews and my assumptions.


Quantitive input

At the same time, I looked at data dashboard (Amplitude) to understand the problem from a quantitive perspective.

% of users that refine their search by different filters.

From high to low:
Brands 8%, On Sale 6%, Price 3%, Size 3%, New Arrival 0%
% of users that search and refine on different devices.

Take-aways

  1. Users are using some filters more often than the others. Generally speaking, users refine their search by brands and sales more than size.

  2. In terms of devices, our users search on desktop and mobile, but there are more users who refine their search on desktop.


Taxonomy tree

To better understand what products are available for the users to search and how Spring organize all product categories. I took a close look at Spring's taxnomony tree.

Combining the research of taxonomy tree and user interviews, I found out there were a few pitfalls in our taxonomy tree, which made some product less searchable/discoverable.

Party of Spring taxonomy tree.

Women's and Men's are kind of similar to each other, which means there are products that exist under both Women's and Men's.
Taxonomy tree structure is not ideal

Women's and Men's are kind of similar to each other, which means there are products that exist under both Women's and Men's.

Take-aways

  1. There are some gender neutral products that are under multiple categories, e.g. jeans, tops, and sneakers.

  2. The taxonomy tree structure is not ideal, sometimes, users cannot tell whether there are sub-categories or not.

User flow

There are multiple entry points for Search. Before I officially start any design, I mapped out all the possible user flows of search, so that I can take all possible scenarios into consideration.

Sketch of all possible user flows

I included one engineer into this user flow sketch session. With her techincal input, I was able to simplify the user flows into 3 different senarios.

There are 3 different entry points of search and refine experience:

  1. Nav bar -> Search grid -> Refinement

  2. Search bar -> Query search -> Search grid -> Refinement

  3. Editorial -> Search grid -> Refinement
