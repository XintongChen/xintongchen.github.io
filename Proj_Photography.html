Photography Composition

A website to teach how to use Golden Ratio in photo composition

  Problem

Nowadays, people take photos everywhere, everyday. However, not everyone could take good photos. Sometimes, it is because their photos compose bad. There are too many ways to do excellent composition. Among them, using Golden Ratio is one of the most effective ways to make photos harmonious, balanced and attractive.

  Solution

This website will (1) illustrate basic rules to use Golden Ratio in composition, (2) help learners recognize Golden Ratio patterns in photos and experience differences between Golden Ratio photos and non-Golden Ratio photos.

  Design Document

SCOPE::Content Illustration of three composition rules (Golden Section, Golden Triangle and Golden Spiral) which explains how to use Golden Ratio when taking photos, from easy to hard, via multimedia with interaction. In addition, 5 multiple-choice questions, which helps learners strengthen their understanding of the learning content. Each question is a contrasting case, including a Golden Ratio photo and a non-Golden Ratio photo. Text hints and image hints are provided for each question.

STRUCTURE::Interactivity When learning rules of applying Golden Ratio, learners could choose to show or hide “golden” grid over photos, which makes the abstract concept more concrete and easy to be understood. When doing multiple-choice quiz, learners could choose to see text or image hints if they need scaffolding to support them to finish the quiz. Besides, system will give feedback to learners’ choices or go to the next question according to learners’ control. (But, learners could not go to the next question, if they do not make a right choice in the current question.)

STRUCTURE::InformationThere are three pages: (1) an intro page serves to simply describe what is Golden Ratio, as well as, to attract and motivate learners to get involved in learning about Golden Ratio in composition, (2) an instruction page to illustrate three “golden” rules, (3) a quiz page including 5 multiple-choice questions and corresponding hints. Users could go back to the Intro page from any other pages, and go to any other pages directly from the Intro page.

DESIGN RATIONALE When illustrating how to apply three “golden” rules, both text and image descriptions are used to make abstract rules more concrete and easy to be understood. The image is a more obvious, and the text provides more detailed information. Besides, in the quiz section, learners are actually analyzing sets of contrasting cases (Golden Ratio photo vs. non-Golden Ration photo), which can help them become more sensitive to information or pattern that they may miss otherwise (Schwartz & Bransford, 1998). After the quiz, a strong pattern of “Golden rules” will be built in learner’s mind.
Schwartz, D. L., & Bransford, J. D. (1998). A time for telling. Cognition and instruction, 16(4), 475-5223.
