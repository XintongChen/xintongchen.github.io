Grovo Case Studies

I've been leading the design work of Grovo Style Guide and Grovo Admin Experience. Here are 2 case studies I wanted to share.

Case study 1: Grovo Style Guide

Problem

The product kept evolving very fast in the past 2 years, and there were multiple designers working on different parts of the product. So it was hard to keep it consistent.

Goal

To build a style guide for product design at Grovo, which allows for better product development cycles and also aims to improve the user experience of the product.

Research

I conducted research and looked into other SaaS products’ style guide and design system. At the same time, I went through every corner of Grovo and figured out what I should cover in this styel guide, and then outlined the structure of our style guide.

final catalog of Grovo style guide

Rules

First of all, I focused on the very basic rules of our product design, including responsive grid system, colors, typography, icons and writing styles.

Conventions

Building a style guide is not all about creating new components or interactions. A lot of times, I looked for the best treatment for each component, built a convention, and clarified when to use this convention.

For example, we use tooltips to provide hints and simple instructions. Even though they were all very well-functioning, but they had either different radio corners, drop-shadows, or paddings:

Sketch templates

The outcome of this project includes a pdf version guidance and a Sketch template with all the components. Designers can grab and use these compnents which improves our efficiency and consistency a lot.

Maintenance

Building style guide is definitely not a one time work. As we are growing our products, the style guide is continually expanding and reshaping as well.

For example, our icon library is growing.

Case study 2: Multi-Language and Closed Caption (CC)

What's Grovo?

Grovo is a B2B SaaS company that delivers engaging Microlearning® lessons to help employees do their jobs better.

Grovo's Microlearning platform has two types of users. For employees (learners), it's an engaging learning space where they take assigned lessons or consume learning content at their own pace. For clients and admins, it's a powerful tool that allows them to create, assign and manage content, as well as track learners' progress.

User Needs & Business Needs

User Needs: Some of Grovo's enterprise customers have overseas employees who are non-English speakers and work outside of U.S. These overseas employees want to learn Grovo lessons in their own languages.

Business Needs: As Grovo's partnership continues to expand, we needed to support additional languages for potential multinational corporations.


Goal & Scope

After talking to Engineering, Sales, and Customer Success teams, as well as an external translation agency, we decided for the first step of this project that we will support:

1. translation of text content in each lesson into Spanish, French, German and Portuguese;

2. CC of video content in each lesson in Spanish, French, German and Portuguese.


Design & Iteration

We provided users the option to set up their language and CC preferences through their user profile or lesson player.
